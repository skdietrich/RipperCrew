The City That Bleeds

In the underbelly of Chicago, where neon bleeds into shadow and the city's pulse thumps with 700 murders a year, the Chicago Ripper Crew carved a scar that still festers. [cite_start]From 1981 to 1982, Robin Gecht and his disciples turned a red Dodge van into a specter and an attic on North Natchez Avenue into a slaughterhouse[cite: 77, 78].

"Reboot" is an excavation. Through the eyes of Detective Sarah Novak, we re-enter a city paralyzed by the Satanic Panic. [cite_start]While the press screamed about cults, Gecht's crew was operating a "chapel" of wire, knives, and axes—a place where at least 17 suspected victims met their fate, though only a fraction would see justice in court[cite: 79, 84].

The Detective's Burden

Detective Sarah Novak is the lens through which we view this horror. Driven by the unsolved 1972 murder of her sister Jenny, Novak's obsession with the "Red Van" broke the case when the system failed. While the media focused on Satanic Panic, Novak focused on the dust in the wounds—asbestos that pointed directly to an attic on Natchez Avenue.

"We got 'em. But looking at the photos of Linda, Rose, and Shui... justice feels like a bandage on a bullet wound."

[cite_start]Novak's work unified the disparate cases—the ax murder in Lincoln Park, the wire mutilations on the West Side, and the gangland-style shooting in Humboldt Park—proving they were all the work of one crew[cite: 2026].
