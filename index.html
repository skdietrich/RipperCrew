The City That Bleeds

In the underbelly of Chicago, where neon bleeds into shadow and the city's pulse thumps with 700 murders a year, the Ripper Crew carved a scar that still festers. From 1981 to 1982, Robin Gecht and his disciples turned a red Dodge van into a specter and an attic on North Natchez Avenue into a slaughterhouse.

The Mastermind // Age: 27

Edward Spreitzer

The Muscle // Age: 20

A hulking, dim-witted laborer seeking approval. Described as Gecht's "loyal dog," his physical strength was weaponized to subdue victims in the van. His virginity and social awkwardness were exploited by Gecht to fuel rage.


Weapon: Brute force, .38 Revolver (Drive-by).

Andrew Kokoraleis

The Chaos // Age: 18

Volatile and acne-scarred, Andrew thrived on the adrenaline of the kill. Unlike his brother, he showed no remorse, treating the torture as sport. His knife work was described as frenzied and chaotic.


Status: Executed (1999).

The Hunter: Sarah Novak

Detective Sarah Novak is a woman forged by loss. Haunted by the 1972 unsolved murder of her sister Jenny, she views the Ripper case not just as a job, but as a crusade. While the real CPD dismissed victims as "hookers" and "runaways," Novak connects the dots between the piano wire marks and the attic dust.

"They aren't just killing. They are performing. The attic isn't a hideout; it's a stage. And Gecht is the director."

From the forensic breakthrough of identifying candle wax in the wounds to the high-stakes raid on Natchez Avenue, Novak's narrative arc bridges the gap between the cold facts of history and the emotional devastation of the victims' families. She is the lens through which we see the cracks in the crew‚ÄîThomas's guilt, Andrew's madness, and Gecht's absolute depravity.
