CHAPTER 1: CONTEXT

THE CITY THAT BLEEDS

"Downtown, the Sears Tower gleamed like a middle finger to the heavens... but beyond the skyscrapers, Chicago was bleeding."

Spring, 1981. Chicago was a paradox of ambition and decay. While bankers in the Loop ignored the screeching El trains, a red Dodge van haunted the West Side. The city's murder rate was climbing toward 979 a year[cite: 37].

This reboot isn't just a retelling; it's a forensic excavation. Through the eyes of Detective Sarah Novak—driven by the 1972 murder of her sister Jenny—we re-enter a city blinding itself to the monsters in its midst.

PERPETRATOR PROFILES

THE PACK

ROBIN GECHT

ROLE: The Mastermind

Age 28. Handyman. Worked for John Wayne Gacy's PDM Contractors in 1978[cite: 134]. The charismatic leader whose attic "chapel" on North Natchez Ave became the epicenter of the horror. Driven by a fetish for blood and domination.

EDWARD SPREITZER

ROLE: The Muscle

Age 21. A hulking laborer with a slow mind, easily manipulated by Gecht's "god-like" persona. His physical strength was used to subdue victims in the van[cite: 196].

ANDREW KOKORALEIS

ROLE: The Chaos

Age 19. The "wild card." Known for a cackling demeanor during assaults. His knife fetish mirrored Gecht's, making him a willing and eager participant in the mutilations[cite: 200].

THOMAS KOKORALEIS

ROLE: The Weak Link

Age 22. Andrew's brother. Plagued by guilt and religious repression (Greek Orthodox). His hesitation—and a singular act of mercy—would eventually crack the case wide open[cite: 224].

MEMORIAL ARCHIVE

HONOR THEIR LIGHT

The media called them "victims." This archive remembers them as people. Daughters, sisters, and dreamers whose lights were stolen but never extinguished.

LINDA SUTTON

MAY 23, 1981

The first vanishing. Abducted from Division Street. Her murder marked the escalation from assault to ritual mutilation. Found in a vacant lot near the Chicago River[cite: 227].

LORRAINE BOROWSKI

MAY 15, 1982

The Butterfly. A secretary at Remax Realty in Elmhurst. She was abducted while walking to work. Her collection of pinned butterflies became a tragic symbol of her fragile, stolen life[cite: 859].

ROSE BECK DAVIS

SEPT 9, 1982

The Rising Star. A marketing executive in Lincoln Park. Abducted after a client dinner. Her murder introduced a new, brutal weapon to the crew's arsenal: the ax[cite: 2036].

SHUI MAK

JUNE 13, 1982

The heart of her family's Chinatown diner. Abducted while walking to her bus stop. Her resilience in the face of the crew's horror was a silent testament to her strength[cite: 1280].

SANDRA DELAWARE

AUG 28, 1982

A survivor of the West Side streets who worked as a bartender. Snatched near the Blue Moon dive bar. Her sharp tongue and fighter's spirit were silenced in the attic chapel[cite: 1324].

RAFAEL & ALBERTO

OCT 6, 1982

The Outliers. Victims of a drive-by shooting in Humboldt Park—a "side hustle" for the crew. Rafael Tirado died; Alberto Rosario survived, his testimony adding to the confusion[cite: 2311].

EVIDENCE LOCKER

ITEM 001: THE RED VAN

A battered red Dodge. The mobile cage where initial assaults took place. Identified by survivor Beverly Washington via a partial plate: "7".

ITEM 002: THE WIRE

Piano wire. Gecht's signature tool for mutilation. Used on Linda Sutton and Angel York to exact surgical, perimortem wounds[cite: 1712].

ITEM 003: THE AX

Rusted, heavy. Introduced in the murder of Rose Beck Davis. Rust flakes found in the wounds linked the weapon to the damp attic conditions of the Chapel[cite: 2165].

THE SURVIVORS

Beverly Washington: Abducted Oct 8, 1982. She survived the wire and the knife. Her testimony provided the critical clue—the description of the "chapel" and the partial plate—that led Sarah Novak to Gecht's door[cite: 2641].

Angel York: Abducted June 1982. Discarded in an alley, she crawled to a payphone. Her scars told the story the police initially ignored[cite: 1370].

DETECTIVE SARAH NOVAK

"I'm not letting you fade. You're not forgotten."

Fictional Lead Investigator. Forged in the trauma of her sister Jenny's 1972 murder. Sarah represents the collective effort of the real detectives (Hein, Wilkosz) who chased the van into the dark[cite: 62].
